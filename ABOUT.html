Debut History

Kwon Ji-yong (Korean: 권지용; born August 18, 1988),best known as G-Dragon (지드래곤), is a South Korean rapper, singer, songwriter, and entrepreneur, known as the "King of K-pop".Born and raised in Seoul, South Korea, G-Dragon made his debut in 2006 and rose to prominence as the leader of the South Korean boy band BigBang, which went on to become one of the best-selling boy bands in the world.

In 1993, G-DRAGON became a child star in the children's variety show BOBOBO. In 2001, he became addicted to Hip-Hop after hearing Wu-Tang Clan's song "CREAM". In the same year, G-DRAGON participated in a special album titled Korean Hip-Hop Flex. In 2006, he made his debut as the leader of the group BIGBANG. In 2009, G-DRAGON released his debut album Heartbreaker. In December of the same year, G-DRAGON held his first solo concert "Shine a Light" at the Seoul Olympic Park, attracting 24,000 people.

In 2013, G-DRAGON performed his "ONE OF A KIND" solo world tour. In May 2015, G-DRAGON officially released the album "MADE SERIES" with the group BIGBANG, and as of July, the total number of views of the five MV videos exceeded 173 million. In 2017, G-DRAGON performed the "ACT III, M.O.T.T.E" world Tour. In 2009, G-DRAGON's debut album Heartbreaker won the Korean MAMA Award for Best Album of the Year. In 2010, G-DRAGON won the Cyworld Music Award for Best Singer. In 2013, G-DRAGON won the 27th Korea Golden Record Awards for his song "Crayon" and the CeCi Popularity Award. In the same year, he won four awards at the Korean MAMA Awards for Best Singer of the Year, Best Male Singer, Best Male Dance Performance, and Best Music Video for his second music album COUP D'ETAT. In 2011, according to several Korean media reports, G-DRAGON was investigated by prosecutors on suspicion of smoking marijuana. In December 2023, the Incheon Police Agency announced that G-DRAGON had been cleared of suspicion on December 18 and would not be handed over to prosecutors.
